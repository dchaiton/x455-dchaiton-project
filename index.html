The Top 10 Most Scenic Hikes in Banff for Families

With turquoise lakes, gentle forest paths, and wildlife around every bend, Banff National Park is an unforgettable playground for families. Whether you're traveling with toddlers, teens, or grandparents, these scenic hikes and adventures offer manageable distances, rewarding views, and plenty of opportunities to pause, explore, and make lasting Rocky Mountain memories.

1. Johnston Canyon

One of the park's most popular family walks, this easy trail follows catwalks along a dramatic canyon to Lower and Upper Falls. It's stroller-friendly to the Lower Falls and exciting for kids thanks to bridges, rushing water, and tunnel viewpoints.

2. Lake Louise Lakeshore Trail

Flat, wide, and endlessly scenic, this gentle shoreline stroll offers postcard views of turquoise water and towering peaks. Ideal for all ages, with plenty of space for breaks and photos.

3. Lake Minnewanka Shoreline

Choose a short out-and-back section along this sparkling lake near the Banff townsite. Families can combine a lakeside walk with a picnic or a boat cruise for a full day of adventure.

4. Fenland Trail

A peaceful 2 km loop through forest and marshland, this easy interpretive trail is perfect for younger kids. Watch for birds, deer, and interpretive signs along the way.

Trip Ideas

Banff, nestled in the majestic Canadian Rockies within Alberta, Canada, is not just a haven for solo adventurers and couples but also a spectacular destination for families. Offering a unique blend of breathtaking natural beauty, wildlife viewing, and family-friendly activities, Banff is an ideal spot for creating lasting memories with your loved ones. Banff National Park is the main attraction, providing a playground vast enough to have something for every member of the family. With over 6,500 square kilometers of awe-inspiring wilderness, the park offers numerous opportunities for families to connect with nature and each other. Start with easy trails such as the Fenland Trail or the Bow River Loop, perfect for little legs and strollers. For families with older children, the hike up to Johnston Canyon to see the waterfalls is thrilling and perfectly doable. The town itself is welcoming and easy to navigate, with plenty of casual dining options, interactive museums, and cozy accommodations. Whether it's building snowmen in winter, discovering turquoise lakes in summer, or simply enjoying the fresh mountain air, Banff provides unforgettable experiences that bring families closer together.

Often called the “Castle in the Rockies,” the Fairmont Banff Springs is one of Canada's most iconic hotels, providing luxury and history in a stunning, castle-like setting. It offers a full-service spa, numerous dining options, and easy access to outdoor activities. Located close to downtown Banff, the Banff Park Lodge offers comfortable lodging with convenient access to the town's shops, restaurants, and attractions. It features spacious rooms and excellent amenities, including an indoor pool, sauna, and steam room, making it a great family-friendly option. Whether you're sipping afternoon tea with panoramic mountain views or setting out on an alpine adventure, the hotel provides a timeless escape where grandeur and nature meet in perfect harmony. The closest major airport is Calgary International Airport, about 1.5 hours' drive from Banff. Car rentals and shuttle services are readily available, providing easy access to the park.
